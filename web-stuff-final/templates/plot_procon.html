    Australian energy balance – consumption vs production

    • Production: Australia is a substantial net exporter of energy, including coal and natural gas, Western Australia remained Australia’s largest producer of natural gas.
    • Black coal production grew by 2 per cent in 2018–19 and remained Australia’s largest contributor to energy production.
    • Australia has seen a long-term decline in production of crude oil and natural gas liquids in the last decade as mature fields decline, but the opening of new natural gas liquids production in the north west took Australian production back to the level seen three years ago.
    • Consumption: Wind and solar were the other major sources of growth contributing to renewable energy consumption.
    • In 2018–19, petroleum products, natural gas and electricity accounted for more than half of Australia’s final energy consumption.
    • Coal remained the second largest fuel consumed however it is declining largely due to lower brown and black coal-fired electricity generation, which was displaced by renewable generation.
